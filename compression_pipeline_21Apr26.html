Approach

Encoding Evaluation & Final Pipeline

Three encoders evaluated on the same source footage; SVT-AV1 selected on the size/quality trade-off

Source: H.264 · 1920×1080 · 25 fps
Target: SVT-AV1 · 854×480 · 2 fps · no audio
Goal: cloud-storable, inference-ready footage
Apple VideoToolbox
Hardware H.265
Fast, low-CPU encoding on Apple Silicon. Good real-time throughput but coarser rate-distortion at aggressive bitrates.
libx265
Software H.265
Mature software HEVC encoder. Competitive quality, but higher bitrate than AV1 at matched visual quality for our footage.
SVT-AV1
Software AV1
Highest compression efficiency at 480p / 2fps in this evaluation. Retains driver/cabin detail at ~4% of the original bitrate — yields the 96% size reduction.

Final Pipeline

Raw H.264 · 1080p · 25fps
→
Downscale to 854×480
→
Resample to 2 fps
→
Drop audio
→
SVT-AV1 encode
Why SVT-AV1 at 2 fps: the DMS model operates on sparse sampled frames — 2 fps retains every decision window at a fraction of the original bitrate. Audio is dropped as an unused channel. Final footprint fits fleet-scale cloud archival.

Key Points

  • Raw 1080p / 25 fps H.264 footage was too large for efficient cloud storage and AI inference
  • Evaluated VideoToolbox (H.265), libx265 (H.265), and SVT-AV1 on the same clips
  • SVT-AV1 at 480p / 2fps / no audio delivered ~96% reduction while preserving visual fidelity
  • Final dataset covers ~440 hours across ~10 school buses + 1 PT bus
