STEP 1 OF 4

The Expensive Solution: 3D CNNs

2D convolution operates on spatial dimensions only (H×W). 3D convolution adds the temporal axis (H×W×T), requiring 3–10× more computation. Too expensive for edge deployment.

STEP 2 OF 4

The TSM Trick: Channel Shifting

Instead of 3D convolution, TSM shifts a fraction of channels along the temporal axis before each 2D conv layer. Each frame receives features from its neighbors — zero extra FLOPs.

STEP 3 OF 4

Residual Shift: Preserving Spatial Info

In-place shifting overwrites original channel values. Residual shift places the temporal shift inside the residual branch while the identity shortcut preserves the original spatial features.

STEP 4 OF 4

Our Preprocessing Pipeline

NIR frames are extremely dark (88% of pixels below 50/255). CLAHE redistributes contrast, then frames are normalized with ImageNet statistics at full 640×480 resolution.

Complete Architecture

TSM-ResNet50 for 8-class DMS action recognition on Drive&Act NIR video

8 NIR frames at full 640×480 are preprocessed with CLAHE and ImageNet-normalized. TSM inserts temporal shifts in every ResNet-50 residual block. Frame features are mean-pooled, then classified into 8 DMS actions.

Design Decisions

Key choices and their rationale

Class Distribution

Drive&Act 8 DMS classes — significant imbalance addressed with sqrt-inverse-freq weighting

safe_driving dominates at 3,707 clips while looking_around has only 107 (35x imbalance). We use sqrt-inverse-frequency class weighting in the loss function to prevent the model from ignoring minority classes.
