Methodology

Pipeline & Cabin ROI

Three steps. Same rule applies to face and person detectors.

Step 1 / Detect

Run face / person detector

Every detection is a bounding box around a candidate occupant in the frame.

Step 2 / Filter

Cabin ROI test

Drop boxes whose centre lies outside the cabin region. Windshield and side-window areas are excluded.

Step 3 / Assign

Largest box in the cabin = driver

The largest surviving box becomes the driver. The rest are passengers. If nothing survives, the driver is missing.

Example 01 / Lone driver
One person in the cabin. Counted as driver. No passengers.
Example 02 / Multi-occupant
Four people in the cabin. The largest (right) is the driver; the other three are passengers.
Example 03 / ROI drop
Three detections, but two fall outside the cabin and are dropped. Only the in-cabin one is counted.
Why "largest" rather than position-based: camera mounts vary across the fleet, so the driver doesn't always appear on the same side. Largest box generalises — the driver is closest to the camera in every layout.
Known failure: this pipeline fails when there's a passenger but the driver is outside the vehicle — the passenger gets labelled "driver" and the count for passengers drops by one. Further experimentation required to tackle this issue.
