Verdict. Fallback contamination is a real bug but not the dominant explanation for the bad fatigue numbers. Filtering to face-only frames moves the leaderboard F1 by ±1–2 points for most drowsiness classifiers — not enough to change any model ranking. The bigger issues exposed by this rescore are (a) tiny GT support (only 27/626 fatigue positives), (b) systematic GT-distribution skew between face-cropped and fallback subsets, and (c) three saturated classifiers that are broken regardless of crop. The fallback fix is worth doing for correctness — but it won't make a good model out of a bad one.

The surprising finding — fallback frames have systematically different GT

A face-only rescore implicitly assumes that face-cropped and fallback frames are random samples from the same distribution. They aren't. The fallback subset is heavily enriched in distracted_driving (the driver looking away is precisely what causes both the face detection to fail and the GT label to fire), and slightly depleted in fatigue:

This shift explains the counterintuitive specialist regressions. gaze_l2cs_net's distracted_driving F1 was 0.558 across all frames; on face-only it drops to 0.501. The drop isn't because the model got worse on faces — it's because the easy-positive frames (driver clearly looking sideways → full cabin frame → high yaw → fires & correct) have been removed from the test set. The "leaderboard F1" was effectively averaging over two different evaluation tasks and face-only isolates the harder one.

Why didn't the F1 numbers move more?

Tiny support is the dominant constraint

Fatigue has only 27 positive frames in the entire 626-frame Hikvision review set. On the 178-frame fallback subset, fatigue support is 5. With support that small, F1 is governed by false-positive rate, which is roughly equal across both regimes for the drowsiness classifiers (they fire too often everywhere). Filtering frames doesn't change that.

For F1 to actually move, we'd need either (a) per-frame thresholding tuned per regime, or (b) a much larger validation set where face vs fallback discrimination starts to matter at the precision level.

Three classifiers are saturated, not face-degraded

The teen_d_* family fires prob = 1.0 on every single frame — face or fallback, Hikvision or anywhere else. The earlier per-prob audit shows this clearly: their face-only prob mean and fallback prob mean are both 1.0000. This is a model-wrapper or head-transplant bug, independent of the crop pipeline. The face-only F1 lift (0.083 → 0.094) is purely a numerator artifact: the face-only subset has higher fatigue prevalence (4.9% vs 4.3%), so a 100%-fires classifier's recall-bounded F1 goes up.

The mosesb_mobilevitv2 paradox

From the earlier audit: this model fires fatigue at 14% on face crops but 95% on fallback — the most extreme prob skew of any model. Yet its F1 barely moves (0.054 → 0.047). Why?

Because the 178 fallback frames contain 5/27 = 19% of all fatigue positives, and the model's 95% fire rate on fallback catches all 5. That contribution to recall almost exactly offsets the precision damage from the 169 false-positive fires. The mixed-regime F1 is the average of "model is dumb but rights" and "model is correct but cautious." Face-only removes both effects together — net change is small.

The real lessons

  • Crop fidelity matters at the prediction layer (huge prob skew per the earlier audit) but the F1 metric is too coarse to surface it on a 27-positive validation set.
  • Models that handle fallback gracefully are not necessarily better models — chbh7051 ViT correctly low-confidences the full cabin, but its face-only F1 (0.107) is still too low to deploy.
  • The leaderboard ranking is mostly driven by saturation and threshold choice, not by whether the model received a face crop. The fallback fix is a correctness fix, not a leaderboard-ranking fix.

Updated recommendations

  1. Treat the fallback fix as a correctness/honesty issue, not a metric fix. The leaderboard should mark n_face_only alongside n_frames and provide a face-only metric column. Persisting both numbers gives reviewers visibility without breaking existing comparisons.
  2. Investigate the teen_d saturation bug independently. Three classifiers in the same family all output prob=1.0 — that's almost certainly a preprocessing or head-transplant issue at the wrapper layer (see build_composed_from_pretrained's torchvision branch). Worth a focused debug session.
  3. Expand the Hikvision validation set's positive support. 27 fatigue positives is too few to discriminate good models from bad ones at the F1 layer. Targeting say 100–200 positives per class would let face-only vs all-frames metric deltas reach significance.
  4. For the field test, prioritize models robust to face-detection failure. chbh7051 ViT's "low-confidence on fallback" behavior is the right failure mode for production — better to underfire than to false-alarm a sleepy driver. mosesb_mobilevitv2 and doze_guard's "fire-on-everything-when-uncertain" pattern is the wrong failure mode for an OEM alert system.
