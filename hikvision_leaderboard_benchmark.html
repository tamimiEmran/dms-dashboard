Hikvision baseline row

oem_prediction is synonym-expanded into the 6 canonical classes (same _SYNONYMS dict as build_model_leaderboard.py). For each class we count TP (GT-positive & predicted-positive) and support (all GT-positives). Recall = TP / support. Classes with support = 0 in this manifest are reported as n/a.

Model rows

Each model runs as a single-detector pipeline (no fusion). Detectors without a native class-mapping for a given canonical are forced to fires=False by design — on this recall view those rows show 0.000 because TP = 0. Per-run TP + support persist to leaderboard.csv and the table below picks the latest run per (model, class).

Why recall ≠ a full ranking

A detector that says “yes” to every frame has recall 1.000 — but also massive false-positive rate. Cross-check with F1 (--view --metric f1) or precision before picking winners. See caveats slide.

Per-class breakdown

Where each class lands

Who beats the OEM baseline, who loses, and the precision/recall trade-off behind the numbers

distracted_driving Hikvision 0.609

Best model: mediapipe at 0.724 (Δ +0.115) — the only row that actually detects distracted_driving and beats the OEM on recall.

Forced-negative everywhere else: 8 rows sit at 0.000 because their class-mapping has nothing pointing to distracted_driving. That’s the capability matrix speaking empirically.

Support = 279 / 619 (45%). Hikvision catches 170; MediaPipe catches 202. Pair with precision before declaring MediaPipe a winner — its threshold-based over-firing is a known trade-off.

eating_drinking Hikvision n/a

Every row: n/a.

Why: the leaderboard’s _SYNONYMS maps drinking_eating to distracted_driving and leaves eating_drinking empty, so no frame ends up as a positive for this class.

Unblocks as soon as we either (a) add eating/drinking synonyms to the leaderboard’s expansion map, or (b) benchmark on OEM Raqeeb data where the taxonomy has a dedicated eating label.

fatigue Hikvision 0.889

Perfect recall: classifier_teen_d_mobilenetv2 and _resnet18 at 1.000 (27/27, Δ +0.111). They catch every drowsy GT frame.

But: both models previously scored 0.043 on the overall-accuracy view — because they fire drowsy on nearly every cabin frame. Their precision is terrible; they trade FP for FN. mediapipe matches Hikvision exactly (24/27, Δ 0).

classifier_doze_guard at 0.852 (23/27) is the most balanced face-crop classifier. classifier_chbh7051_vit under-fires at 0.185 (5/27) — opposite failure mode.

phone_usage Hikvision 0.514

No model beats Hikvision. Best is yolo_soham at 0.009 (1/111).

Chaitanya/Soham under-fire massively. Both are DMS-specific YOLOs with a phone class, but their confidence threshold (0.50) is too strict for Hikvision’s low-light cabin shots.

Support = 111 / 619 (18%). Lower the confidence threshold, add SAHI slicing, or bring in RT-DETR / YOLO-NAS / YOLO11x COCO rows — all queued in the roster. This is the column most likely to unlock with more models.

seatbelt Hikvision 0.806

Perfect recall: yolo_chaitanya at 1.000 (217/217, Δ +0.194). yolo_soham a close second at 0.945 (205/217, Δ +0.138).

Caveat same as fatigue: Chaitanya’s invert_classes=["seatbelt"] flips presence into absence, and it over-fires missing-seatbelt on a lot of frames — its accuracy (TP+TN/total) on this class was 0.422. Pair with precision.

Support = 217 / 619 (35%). DMS-specific YOLO detectors beat the OEM decisively on recall. seatbelt_v4_onnx is wired to drop in next and should give us a third independent row.

smoking Hikvision n/a

Every row: n/a.

Why: zero smoking positives in the 619-frame review manifest.

The column is uninformative until either (a) more frames with smoking labels land, or (b) we benchmark on OEM Raqeeb / Howen which have smoking events. Catalogued as the persistent gap — no base-COCO model covers cigarettes, so we’re also waiting on a fine-tuned smoking classifier row.

Caveats

Reading the table honestly

Three warnings before anyone reads recall as a leaderboard ranking

Recall alone

1.000 can be bad

A detector that always says “yes” has recall = 1.000. TEEN-D MobileNetV2/ResNet18 hit perfect recall on fatigue (27/27) partly because they over-fire on every cabin frame — previously their overall accuracy was 0.043. Always pair recall with precision or F1. Use --view --metric f1 or --view --metric precision on the same leaderboard CSV.

Support = 0

n/a is not a zero

The eating_drinking and smoking columns show n/a because the 619-frame manifest has no GT positives for those classes. Any displayed number would be division-by-zero. Don’t reward models that don’t fire there — we simply can’t tell on this manifest.

Forced-negative

0.000 with support > 0 is information

A row at 0.000 on a class with real support isn’t “broken” — it’s empirical evidence that the row’s class mapping doesn’t reach that canonical. That’s the capability matrix’ “bad” cell proven by data. Eight of the nine rows show this pattern for distracted_driving, phone, fatigue (for detection rows), or seatbelt (for classifier rows).
